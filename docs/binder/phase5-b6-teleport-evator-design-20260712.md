## Concept (Peetsa's idea, recorded 2026-07-10, green-lit 2026-07-12)

The **teleport-evator** is the *invisible* upgrade to the B-5 crossing. Where B-5 is a framed, consensual
trip (fog → "pass through?" prompt → curtain → arrival title), the evator has **no prompt, no curtain, no
title**: you walk (or fly) toward the E/W edge and the world simply *continues* — on the other hemisphere.

The trick that makes it invisible: the outermost few degrees of longitude at each E/W edge are generated as
a **mirror image of the opposite edge**, so that a one-shot teleport across the seam drops you into terrain
that is *identical* to what you were just standing in. No hitch, no ceremony — an invisible seam.

**This is NOT the scrapped seamless wrap** (see the dedicated landmine walk-through below). The three
structural differences: (1) terrain is *genuinely* identical because we mirror the GENERATION, not fake a
density sink; (2) the teleport is ONE-SHOT at a trigger line, never per-tick; (3) B-5's already-shipped
mirror-teleport engine (`HemispherePassageService`) does the move — we extend it, we do not re-invent it.

## The mirror geometry (the load-bearing math)

### Reflection, not translation — and why

Let valid world-X be `[−R, +R]` where `R = intendedXRadius` (`util/LatitudeMath.java:91`, i.e. `zRadius`
in Classic, `zRadius * MERCATOR_ASPECT` in Wide — `MERCATOR_ASPECT = 2.0`,
`world/LatitudeBiomes.java:793`). The border is centered at `(0,0)` (`GlobeMod.java:335-360`).

For an edge-to-edge teleport to be seamless, the player's *local neighborhood* at the source must match the
destination's. At the **east** edge (`x = +R`) the wall is on the `+X` side and terrain falls away toward
`−X`; at the **west** edge (`x = −R`) the wall is on the `−X` side and terrain falls away toward `+X`. For
the jump `+R → −R` to be imperceptible we therefore need, for every inward depth `d ∈ [0, W]`:

```
terrain(+R − d)  ==  terrain(−R + d)      (both measured INWARD from their own wall)
```

That is a **reflection about the world center**: `x ↦ −x`. It is *exactly* B-5's `HemispherePassage.mirrorX`
(`2*centerX − x`, `core/HemispherePassage.java:131`, `= −x` at center 0). Reflection — not a pure
translation-glue — is what makes both walls line up AND lets us reuse the shipped mirror math verbatim.

Peetsa described a "constant X-offset." Honest reconciliation: `x ↦ −x` is a reflection whose *delta*
`(−2x)` is not globally constant, but at the trigger line `x ≈ ±R` it is a near-constant jump of `≈ ∓2R`.
So "constant offset near the edge" is true; the underlying map is the reflection the shipped code already
performs. (A true translation-glue — genuine cylinder periodicity `terrain(x) == terrain(x−2R)` everywhere
— IS the scrapped seamless wrap and is explicitly off the table.)

### Copy direction: WEST is canonical, EAST is the mirror

We enforce the reflection asymmetrically, which also minimizes the blast radius:

- **WEST inner band `[−R, −R + W]` = CANONICAL.** Generated exactly as the mod generates it today. Byte-
  identical to current output — the west edge does not change at all.
- **EAST inner band `[+R − W, +R]` = MIRROR COPY.** Generated by remapping the world-X coordinate to its
  reflection `−x` (which lands in the canonical west band) at every X-entry chokepoint. Because the west
  band is generated normally, the east band becomes its exact reflection with *zero* per-noise-function
  changes — the remap rides through every X-keyed noise call downstream.

So only east-edge chunks differ from today's Latitude output; west-edge chunks stay byte-identical. The
teleport always lands you on canonical (west) terrain, and by construction the east edge you left matches it.

### The unavoidable "echo" and the inner-frontier seam — and why the fog exists

Two honest consequences of a hard-bordered *fake* cylinder (as opposed to the impossible global-periodic
real one):

1. **The echo.** Walking `+X` off the east edge, you land at `−R` still heading `+X`, and walk into
   `terrain(−R + d) == terrain(+R − d)` — i.e. you re-traverse a *mirror* of the band you just crossed.
   For a `W ≈ 5°` band this is ~100–550 blocks of "seen it before, flipped" terrain (see width table). It
   sits almost entirely inside the storm fog, so it is largely unseen.
2. **The inner-frontier seam.** The mirror band must butt against normal interior terrain somewhere (at
   `x = ±(R − W)`). That join is C0-discontinuous in general (mirror value vs. normal value). **This is the
   seam the fog exists to hide.** The B-5 storm/approach fog is thickest across exactly this band
   (`RAMP_START_DEG = 176.5`, climax at the prompt line — `core/EdgeGeometry.java:70`,`147-155`), so the
   fog is not masking a *load hitch* here (as it was in B-5), it is masking a *terrain discontinuity* at the
   band's inner edge. This is the elegant re-use: same fog, new job.

### Band width `W`: recommend `[175°, 180°]` (5°), floored on tiny worlds

`EdgeGeometry.blocksPerDegree = xRadius / 180` (`core/EdgeGeometry.java:98`). Reconciling Peetsa's "~5 deg"
with the shipped feature ramp (`RAMP_START_DEG = 176.5`): set the mirror band inner frontier at **175°**
(1.5° equatorward of the fog onset, so the frontier seam is *already inside* the fog when it first appears).

| World (X radius) | blocks/deg | 5° band `W` | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Itty Classic (3750) | 20.8 | ~104 blk | needs a block floor; frontier ~104 blk from wall |
| Small Classic (5000) | 27.8 | ~139 blk | |
| Regular Classic (7500) | 41.7 | ~208 blk | |
| Small Wide/Mercator (15000) | 83.3 | ~417 blk | |
| Regular Wide/Mercator (20000) | 111.1 | ~555 blk | |

Recommend a `MirrorGeometry` pure core class (sibling of `EdgeGeometry`) that resolves `W` in blocks per
world from the 175°/180° degree anchors with a small-world floor, anchored to `intendedXRadius` (NOT the
live border — inherit the whole reason `EdgeGeometry` exists: the TEST-86 lerping-border finding,
`core/EdgeGeometry.java:7-16`). Unit-tested in the plain JVM like its sibling.

## The X-entry chokepoints — honest per-layer feasibility

The mirror is a **coordinate remap** applied when `x` is in the east band: `x_gen = −x`. Its reach is only
as good as our ability to intercept X at each layer where terrain is decided. Recon of where X enters:

### 1. Biome selection — CHEAP (mechanical)
`LatitudeBiomeSource.getNoiseBiome(x,y,z)` (`world/LatitudeBiomeSource.java:105-121`) is the sole biome
chokepoint; it computes `blockX = x << 2` and hands it to `LatitudeBiomes.pick(..., blockX, blockZ, ...)`
(`world/LatitudeBiomes.java:3005/3790`). Everything downstream is a pure function of `blockX` — the confetti
`VARIANT_CELL` noise, `warmProvinceClass(blockX,...)`, `subtropicalHumidityNoise(blockX,blockZ)`,
`ValueNoise2D.sampleBlocks(seed, blockX, blockZ, scale)`, band jitter, dither (dozens of sites, e.g.
`:79,:82,:208,:321,:870`). **Remap once, at the top of `getNoiseBiome`**: if the (block-scale) X is in the
east mirror band, negate it about the border center before the `pick` call. Every X-keyed noise then
produces the west band's value automatically. One guarded remap; no per-site edits. Also covers the atlas
path (`dev/BiomePreviewExporter.java:255-261` calls the same `pick`), which is what makes the proof possible.

### 2. Terrain heightmap / density — HARD (the make-or-break spike)
This is the risky layer. The rendered surface is the zero-crossing of the vanilla/Terralith `finalDensity`
(#12) graph, sampled by `NoiseChunk` in absolute world coords. The mod already wraps `finalDensity` (and its
`preliminarySurfaceLevel` #11 companion) via `terrain/TerrainRouterWrapping.java` +
`mixin/terrain/RandomStateRouterTerrainMixin.java` — but `GeoTerrainBiasFunction`
(`terrain/GeoTerrainBiasFunction.java:333-446`) only *biases* toward the geo land-field and reads
`ctx.blockX()` for its own sample; the BASE vanilla noise inside `delegate.compute(ctx)` uses `ctx` we do
not currently rewrite, and terrainV2 is default-OFF anyway (`core/LatitudeV2Flags.java:63`).

To mirror the terrain *shape*, the band columns must feed a **reflected `blockX`** into the ENTIRE
`finalDensity` (and `preliminarySurfaceLevel`) graph — a context-remapping `DensityFunction` wrapper
installed through the existing `TerrainRouterWrapping` seam, applied for east-band columns only. This is
delicate: (a) density is sampled in batched `NoiseChunk` fills, so the remap must survive `fillArray`
(the SimpleFunction per-cell `compute` path exists, but batching + the cache/interpolation substitution in
`mapChildren` — see `GeoTerrainBiasFunction.java:483-505` — must be honored); (b) heightmap, aquifer, and
spawn all re-sample the same graph and must see the SAME remapped X or they desync; (c) the frontier at
`x = R − W` must switch remap on/off cleanly (this IS the masked inner seam).

**This wrapper brushes the scrapped-mixin zone but is categorically different** (see landmines §): it remaps
the INPUT coordinate of the existing, real density graph — it does not fabricate a fake sink or a phantom
ocean. It is honest terrain generated at a reflected location. Still, it is the single highest-risk item and
should be built as a dedicated, headless-provable spike before anything else in P1.

### 3. Surface rules (block palette) — MEDIUM
Grass/sand/snow/stone banding is biome+Y+noise driven. If biome (layer 1) and heightmap (layer 2) already
mirror, and the surface-noise sampling inherits the same reflected X (it runs inside the same chunk-gen
context), the palette mostly mirrors. Some `SurfaceSystem` noise is sampled in world coords independently;
minor palette speckle mismatch inside the fog is acceptable for the MVP.

### 4. Feature / decoration placement — INFEASIBLE to mirror cleanly → STRIP (the honest MVP)
This is the deepest problem and it is worth stating plainly. Per-chunk decoration placement seeds off the
REAL chunk origin: vanilla `WorldgenRandom.setDecorationSeed(levelSeed, minBlockX, minBlockZ)`, keyed to the
chunk's actual min corner, invoked inside `ChunkGenerator.applyBiomeDecoration` (the very method the mod
already mixes into — `mixin/ChunkGeneratorGenerateFeaturesBiomeSetMixin.java:121-231`). Two dead ends:

- Substituting the *mirror* chunk's origin into the decoration seed makes the east band draw the same RNG
  as the west chunk — but features place at their own absolute positions via many world-coord RNG draws, so
  you'd get a **translated** copy of the west chunk's decorations sitting on **reflected** terrain: a tree
  born on a west hill lands in an east valley. Terrain and decorations would be mutually incoherent.
- Truly reflecting each feature (mirror every in-chunk offset `dx ↦ 15−dx`, flip every world-coord draw) is
  not generically possible — features self-determine positions through opaque RNG chains.

**Verdict: do not mirror decorations. Strip them from the mirror band.** Extend the already-shipped
structure-free edge belt (`core/EdgeStructureVeto.java` + `mixin/EdgeStructureVetoMixin.java`, its own
default-true worldgen flag, `core/LatitudeV2Flags.java:184`) to also veto surface *features* in the
innermost band. The mirrored terrain SHAPE matches; there are simply no trees/flowers to betray the
reflection. A barren, storm-scoured edge reads naturally as a wind-blasted coast, and it pairs with the
existing veto. This is the pragmatic P1 scope; full decoration reflection is a deferred research spike.

### 5. Carvers / caves — ACCEPTED underground mismatch
Carvers are per-chunk world-coord seeded and will NOT reflect (same class of problem as features). Caves
under a fog-drowned edge band, crossed at the surface, are not visible during the crossing. Accept the
underground mismatch (or optionally veto carvers in the band later). Note the crossing is already surface-
only in B-5 ("underground there's just the world's edge wall") — underground you meet the wall, not the
evator.

**Summary of the honest MVP:** mirror **biome + heightmap** (shape + color — the seamless illusion), **strip
decorations** in the band (barren, fog-masked), **accept** surface-palette speckle and underground/cave
mismatch. The heightmap remap (layer 2) is the critical risk; biome remap and feature-strip are mechanical.

## State model (the trigger + the move)

Silent, one-shot, cooldown-guarded. Reuses B-5's shipped shapes; adds momentum preservation and drops all
ceremony. A new pure core `EvatorCrossing` (sibling of `HemispherePassage`, zero MC imports, unit-tested):

- **PRE-WARM (entering the band, ~177°).** As distance-to-edge drops under a pre-warm line (a touch outside
  the trigger; widen it for elytra speed), the server force-loads the destination 3×3+ ring at the mirror
  target — reuse `HemispherePassageService.resolveArrival` / `GlobeMod.placeSafeY`
  (`HemispherePassageService.java:78-141`). Because the destination terrain is IDENTICAL, even a late chunk
  is a fog-masked non-event; the pre-warm is belt-and-suspenders, not a curtain crutch.
- **TRIGGER (at the wall, ~179.5° / edge contact).** Deeper than B-5's prompt (179°) — the evator fires as
  the player would meet the border, so the crossing happens *at* the wall, landing them at the mirrored
  edge in identical terrain. One-shot with hysteresis: after firing, DISARM; re-arm only after the player
  walks back out past a re-arm line (the `EdgeGeometry`-style asymmetric hysteresis, `HemispherePassage`
  `:56-71`, `REARM_DEG` discipline). A **cooldown** (mirror B-5's 60-tick cross cooldown,
  memory: "60-tick cross cooldown") prevents an immediate bounce-back on the far edge.
- **MOVE — momentum PRESERVED (the invisibility requirement).** Extend the engine with a
  `crossHemisphereMomentum(player)` sibling of `crossHemisphere` (`HemispherePassageService.java:149-166`).
  Differences from B-5's `crossHemisphere`:
  1. Arrival is AT the mirrored edge column (`arrivalAbsX` pulled to the *edge*, not `EdgeGeometry.arrivalDist`
     one degree inland — B-5 lands you past the fog on purpose; the evator lands you in the identical
     mirrored terrain, which is at the edge).
  2. Do NOT zero `deltaMovement` / `fallDistance`. Capture `player.getDeltaMovement()` before the teleport
     and **re-apply it after** so an elytra flight or a sprint carries through the seam without a hitch.
     Keep the `stopRiding` dismount (no mount-carry — same as B-5) unless mount-carry is later designed.
  3. Same safety search + `PROBE_BUDGET` (`:57`) so we never drop into fluid/void.
- **What the player SEES:** identical terrain on both sides + the B-5 storm fog as natural cover. No title,
  no curtain, no prompt. If the far chunk is a tick behind, the fog + identical shape hide it.

**Arrive-in-band is fine here** (unlike B-5, which had to seed disarmed-in-band to avoid self-reprompting):
the evator has no prompt to re-fire, and the cooldown + re-arm hysteresis handle the bounce. The server
still re-validates edge distance on the crossing (anti-exploit, mirror `HemispherePassage.serverAcceptsCross`
`:118-123`) and guards dead/offline/creative between trigger and move.

## What this deliberately does NOT do — the scrapped-wrap landmine walk-through

The seamless E/W wrap of 2026-06-24 is HARD do-not-retry (memory: "E-W WRAPPING attempt SCRAPPED
2026-06-24"). Each of its landmines, and precisely why the evator avoids it:

1. **Per-tick `wrapAndClampTick` teleports.** The scrapped wrap teleported the player every tick as they
   straddled the seam, fighting movement and desyncing. **Evator:** ONE-SHOT at a trigger line with
   disarm + cooldown + asymmetric re-arm hysteresis (the B-5 state machine, `HemispherePassage.java:56-71`).
   Never per-tick.
2. **The fake `LatitudeOceanSinkDensityFunction` / `RandomState` ocean-sink mixin.** The scrapped wrap
   FABRICATED a density sink to force a matching ocean at the seam. It is deleted and must not return (B-5
   design §"does NOT do", `phase5-b5-...-20260710.md:55-57`). **Evator:** the terrain-mirror wrapper remaps
   the INPUT world-X of the REAL, existing `finalDensity` graph for band columns — it generates honest
   Terralith/vanilla terrain at a reflected coordinate. It never invents density, never hacks a sink; a
   `grep` for `OceanSink`/`wrapAndClamp` in this tree returns nothing (confirmed this session).
3. **Multiplayer sightlines across the seam.** A true global wrap promised players could SEE across the
   seam into wrapped terrain — impossible to keep consistent. **Evator:** promises no cross-seam sightline.
   The band is fog-drowned; the far side is a discrete teleport, not a continuous view. Two players never
   share a rendered space across the edge.
4. **False topology promise (a real cylinder).** The scrapped wrap implied genuine global periodicity
   `terrain(x) == terrain(x−2R)` everywhere — which vanilla noise sampled in absolute coords cannot honor,
   and which breaks at every structure/feature/carver. **Evator:** promises only a LOCAL illusion — a thin
   mirrored band + a masked inner-frontier seam + an accepted echo. It is explicitly a *fake* cylinder that
   is honest about its edges, not a claimed real one.

Also inherited from B-5's NOT-list: no seamless illusion is *promised* beyond the fog; cloud-snap on
teleport is accepted; decorations are stripped (not faked); underground/caves are not mirrored.

## Existing worlds (blast radius + recommendation)

Turning the flag on changes generation only within `W` (~5°) of each E/W edge — a band the shipped
`EdgeStructureVeto` (`core/EdgeStructureVeto.java`) has *already* emptied of surface structures, so the
worldgen delta is confined to a thin, structure-poor strip. Still, flipping it on an EXISTING world creates
a generation-frontier tear: new (mirrored) chunks abut old (non-mirrored) chunks *inside* the band. This is
the same documented-accepted upgrade-frontier tear class as the structure veto (memory: "upgrade-frontier
tear documented-accepted").

**Recommendation:** default OFF, and **capture the flag per-world at creation** (the `globeRadius` pattern —
persisted in `LatitudeWorldState`, `world/LatitudeWorldState.java`), so a world born without the evator
NEVER flips even if the project default later changes. Peetsa flies it on a FRESH world first; only after he
blesses it do we consider a default. This mirrors how `passageV2` earned its default-on only after P3.

## Multiplayer

Everything B-5 accepts still holds, plus:
- **A crosses invisibly, B watches:** B sees A vanish at the wall — same as B-5, accepted. With momentum
  preserved, an observer sees A vanish *mid-flight* (elytra), which is if anything cleaner than a hitch.
- **New case — mirrored-band co-occupancy:** player A stands in the `+band` at `+x`, player B stands at the
  mirror `−x` in the `−band`. They are in IDENTICAL-looking terrain at DIFFERENT real coordinates. No
  shared-space illusion is promised (they cannot see or interact across the seam), so this is fine — but it
  is the one genuinely new confusion the evator adds over B-5, worth a line in the honesty notes.
- No new server-authority surface beyond B-5's: the crossing is still server-validated and per-player.

## Open questions for the adversarial sweeper

1. **Reflection vs. translation handedness.** Reflection (`x↦−x`) reuses `mirrorX` and lines up both walls,
   but flips E-W handedness so the echo band reads as a mirror image (rivers/coasts reversed). Is that
   disorienting enough to prefer a translation-copy despite the extra machinery and the loss of the shipped
   `mirrorX` reuse? (Recommendation: reflection — it is what makes the walls match and the engine reuse
   clean; the echo is fog-drowned.) **Peetsa taste call.**
2. **Terrain-shape remap feasibility.** Is a context-remapping `DensityFunction` over `finalDensity` +
   `preliminarySurfaceLevel` actually safe through `NoiseChunk`'s batched `fillArray` + cache/interpolation
   substitution (`GeoTerrainBiasFunction.mapChildren` `:502-505`)? Or does the batch granularity force a
   coarser (per-column) intercept? This is the item most likely to sink the "identical shape" claim.
3. **Fog: stay or go?** Peetsa framed the fog-passage as "the pit stop," but the evator NEEDS the fog to
   mask the inner-frontier seam and any far-chunk pop-in. Recommendation: **keep the storm/approach fog as
   the crossing's weather** (it now earns its keep masking the terrain seam, not a curtain). **Peetsa taste
   call** — if he wants the fog gone, the inner-frontier seam becomes visible and we need a terrain-blend
   there instead (more work, new risk).
4. **Decorations: strip vs. deferred full reflection.** Is a barren mirror band acceptable to Peetsa, or is
   the tree/ore mismatch better than emptiness? (Recommendation: strip for MVP; the band is fog-drowned and
   thin.) Does stripping features (not just structures) in a 100–555 blk band read as "wrong/empty" from
   inside on the larger Wide worlds?
5. **Trigger depth + cooldown vs. bounce.** At an at-the-wall trigger with an at-the-edge arrival, can a
   player oscillate (cross → land at far edge in-band → immediately re-trigger back)? Is the 60-tick
   cooldown + re-arm-past-a-line hysteresis enough at elytra speed, or does the arrival need a small inland
   nudge (contradicting "land in identical terrain")?
6. **Momentum preservation across `teleportTo`.** Does `ServerPlayer.teleportTo(..., true)` within the same
   level preserve `deltaMovement`, or must we re-apply captured velocity — and does re-applied velocity fight
   client-side prediction / cause a rubber-band at the seam? (P2 live-verify.)
7. **Per-world capture mechanism.** Confirm `LatitudeWorldState` is the right persistence home and that a
   world born pre-evator reads flag=false forever even under a later default flip (the `globeRadius`
