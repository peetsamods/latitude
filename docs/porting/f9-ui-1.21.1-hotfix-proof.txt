Scope: F9 settings/HUD Studio parity and screen blur/render-layer authority only

## Problem

The released 1.21.1 backport opened the older/pre-1.3 F9 UI surface. After initial donor restoration, old capture/CSV/dev controls were removed and the HUD Studio surface was restored, but the F9 settings screen and HUD Studio still appeared blurry. The scrollbar itself was blurry, proving this was not just a compass/title overlay issue: the screen content was still being affected by vanilla background/blur layering.

Existing-save loading still uses the vanilla loading screen. That is a separate follow-up and was not touched in this slice.

## Fix

F9 UI parity was restored narrowly:
- F9 opens the Latitude settings front door instead of going directly to HUD Studio.
- Old capture/CSV/dev controls are no longer exposed on the public F9 front door.
- HUD Studio remains reachable from the settings screen.
- `LatitudeSettingsScreen` and `LatitudeHudStudioScreen` now use create-world-style background authority by suppressing the vanilla background/blur pass and letting Latitude own the dim/background layer.
- HUD Studio no longer relies on the vanilla in-game background blur path for its screen layer.

Files touched:
- `src/main/java/com/example/globe/client/LatitudeSettingsScreen.java`
- `src/main/java/com/example/globe/client/LatitudeHudStudioScreen.java`
- `src/main/java/com/example/globe/client/CompassHud.java`
- `src/main/java/com/example/globe/mixin/client/InGameHudMixin.java`
